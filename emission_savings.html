Understanding Emission Savings

The emission savings analysis helps you understand the positive environmental impact of using solar energy instead of traditional grid electricity. Here are some key factors:

  • Annual Energy Production (kWh): This is the total electricity generated by your solar panels each year. It is a crucial factor in calculating the environmental benefits.
  • Emission Factor: The average emission factor for grid electricity is adjustable in the form above. This factor represents the amount of carbon dioxide (CO2) produced when generating electricity from non-renewable sources. The default value is 0.5 kg CO2 per kWh, but you can adjust it to better reflect the conditions in your area.
  • Equivalent Trees Planted: This metric helps you relate your CO2 savings to an equivalent number of trees planted. On average, one tree absorbs approximately 21.77 kg of CO2 per year. The more trees you are equivalent to planting, the larger the positive impact on the environment.

The formula used to calculate the annual CO2 savings is:

Annual CO2 Savings (kg) = Annual Energy Production (kWh) * Emission Factor (kg CO2/kWh)

The equivalent number of trees planted is calculated as:

Equivalent Trees Planted = Annual CO2 Savings (kg) / CO2 Absorption per Tree (21.77 kg per year)

By switching to solar energy, you reduce the demand for fossil fuel-based electricity, thereby decreasing greenhouse gas emissions. This contributes significantly to mitigating climate change and promoting a cleaner environment.
